The problem: lost items cost time, money, and energy

Every school deals with the same daily challenge: students lose things. Water bottles, lunch boxes, jackets, and pencil cases get left behind in classrooms, buses, and playgrounds. Multiply this by hundreds of students, and the school ends up with a mountain of unclaimed items.

Parents spend money replacing the same items again and again. Teachers and staff lose time sorting through lost‑and‑found piles and trying to guess who owns what. Even when items are found, there’s no simple way to connect them back to the right student.

Without a clear system, this problem never really goes away. It quietly drains time, money, and energy from schools and families every single day.

The solution: GulfTag AE makes lost items find their way home

GulfTag AE uses smart QR stickers to connect lost items back to their owners. Instead of relying on handwritten labels that fade or fall off, each student gets QR stickers that can be placed on their belongings. When staff find an item, they simply scan the code and instantly see who it belongs to and how to return it.

Schools choose a plan based on every 3 grade levels, with options for 1, 2, or 5 stickers per student. Once the stickers are in place, the whole school community benefits: staff can return items quickly, parents feel reassured, and students learn responsibility because their belongings are now traceable.

No apps, no accounts, no complicated setup — just a quick registration, a QR code, and a smooth return process.
